# ECNS-560-Term-Project---Claire-Standley
ECNS project examining the effects of agritourism liability laws on direct-to-consumer agricultural markets.


Project Topic: Agritourism Liability Laws and Direct-to-Consumer Agricultural Markets

Topic Motivation: Direct-to-consumer (DTC) agricultural markets, such as farmers markets, on farm stands, and other forms of local food marketing, have grown substantially in the United States over the past decade. These markets allow farmers to capture a larger share of the consumer food dollar and may provide important opportunities for small and beginning farmers who face barriers to entry in traditional agricultural supply chains. Many papers have found that first time farmers and younger farmers are more likely to participate in value added agricultural often time as a means to provide extra income within the first few years to be able to keep the farm operational. One factor that may influence the growth of local food markets is the legal environment surrounding agritourism. Agritourism includes activities such as farm tours, pumpkin patches, corn mazes, and on farm events that may attract visitors to agricultural operations. Other papers have observed clustering effects regarding agritourism and direct to consumer sales in the United States and found these two methods of income to be closely related in most cases. However, farms that host visitors may face liability risks if guests are injured while visiting the property. Starting with Kansas in 2004, many states adopted agritourism liability laws which limit the legal liability of farmers hosting agritourism activities under certain conditions. By reducing the risk of lawsuits, these laws may encourage farmers to expand agritourism activities and attract more visitors. Increased visitor traffic also increases opportunities for farms to sell products directly to consumers. This project will attempt to examine whether the adoption of agritourism liability laws is associated with changes in the development of local food marketing channels, particularly direct to consumer agricultural markets. Understanding the relationship between agritourism policy and local food systems is important for policymakers interested in supporting rural economic development, agricultural diversification, and farm profitability. Additionally, a (better) study of such could provide evidence of an unintended benefit of expansion of limited liability laws to the agricultural community and specifically an opportunity to help with endeavors that can lower barriers to entry for young agriculturists. 

Potential Research Questions: 
1.	Do agritourism liability laws increase the development of direct-to-consumer agricultural markets in the United States? 
2.	Do agritourism liability laws increase the number of farmers markets in counties after the laws are adopted?
3.	Are the effects of agritourism liability laws larger in counties with greater agritourism or tourism related activity?

Variables include market name, city and state, zip code, lat and long, market website, and some vendor information. The timespan in from 2020 to early 2026 being updated periodically. Markets are added over time as they register with the USDA. The datasets include farmers markets across the entire United States, with geographic information that allows markets to be mapped to counties. Each state has several entries as a representative sample. This dataset will allow me to construct a county-by-year measure of the number of farmers markets. Farmers markets serve as a proxy for the development of local marketing channels and direct-to-consumer sales opportunities. The on-farm market data has the same information but spans back to 2014 and includes on farm structures or property that sells products for example farm stands. Census of Agriculture (USDA - National Agricultural Statistics Service - Census of Agriculture)  – Direct-to-consumer Sales – The USDA Census of Agriculture is a comprehensive survey of U.S. farms conducted every 5 years.
